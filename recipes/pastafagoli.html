Pasta Fagoli

Serves 4 - 6


Description

Pasta fagoli can really be anything that encompasses pasta and beans put together and served. Tomato is the most frequent addition. Combine this with onion, celery, fennel and carrot we have a hearty soup that can satisfy all types of cravings. A copious addition of fresh torn kale adds harmonous color and delicate balance. Always served topped with a large amount of extra virgin olive oil, freshly grated parm, and freshly cracked pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb small shaped pasta, such as ditalini
  • 1 & 1/2 cup Milk
  • 1 bunch lacinato kale
  • 1 - 2 large cans Crushed Tomatoes
  • 1 pound of soaked mayocoba beans
  • 3 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1 large Onion
  • 1 bulb Fennel
  • 2 large Carrots
  • 3 ribs of celery
  • 1 teaspoon Oregeno
  • 1 tablespoon Parm
  • 3 Bay Leaf
  • 1 teaspoon Crushed Red Pepper
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Chop or otherwise dice onion, fennel, carrot and celery. Add to pot with two tablespoons of hot oil in medium saucepan over medium heat
  2. Once vegetables are wilted (about 20 minutes), add garlic and fry 2 minutes
  3. Add red pepper, bay leaf, and oregeno to saucepan. Fry for 1 additional minute
  4. Add 10 cups of water and soaked beans, bring to boil, reduce to simmer
  5. After 45 minutes, add crushed tomatoes, stir and bring to simmer. Simmer for an additional 30 minutes
  6. Boil pasta in salted water until no more than al dente, drain, toss with oil and set aside
  7. Add torn kale to soup and allow to wilt on simmer for 5 minutes
  8. Add pasta to boil and top with soup
  9. Top bowl with olive oil, parm and pepper
