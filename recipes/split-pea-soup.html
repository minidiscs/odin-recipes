Split Pea Soup

Serves 4 - 6


Description

A foggy day classic. You don't need to add a ham hock, but it's better with it. Be careful with your salt if your using the hock, as quite a bit salt comes out of the meat. Don't be afraid to do most of your salting near the end of the cook time. Serve with saltine crackers and plenty of freshly cracked pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb green or yellow split peas
  • 1 large spanish onion
  • 1 pound ham hock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 large carrots
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Saut√© chopped onion and chopped carrot in oil over medium head for 10 minutes stirring occasionally
  2. When onion is translucent and slightly browned at the edges and fry for 1 minute. Add hamhock, water and split peas.
  3. Add 8 cups of water. Bring to boil and salt to taste.
  4. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 90 minutes or until smooth and tender. Remove meat from ham bone and add back to soup.
